The Science of AMOLED: Saving Battery

Changing your wallpaper isn't just about aesthetics—it is actually a technical trick to drastically extend your device's daily battery life. But there is a catch: it only works if you have an OLED or AMOLED screen.

1. OLED Technology

Traditional LCD screens use a global backlight. This means even if you have a completely black wallpaper, the backlight is still turned on. However, on modern devices (like the iPhone 15 Pro, Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra), every pixel acts as its own independent lightbulb. When a pixel needs to display "True Black" (Hex code #000000), it completely turns off. It draws zero power.

2. The Battery Saving Math

If you choose a "True Black" Dark Mode asset—like a minimalist neon logo on a pure black background—up to 70% of the pixels on your phone screen are physically turned off. Over a 14-hour day, this can save you a significant percentage of battery life.
